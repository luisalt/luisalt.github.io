I'm Luis Alt.

Over the last decade I’ve been using design to help people and brands to provide better services. Current Ventures

I am the founder of Livework in Brazil - a global innovation and service design studio - and EISE - the first School of Service Innovation in the world. I created the first Design Thinking course in Latin America at Business School ESPM and I teach at Transdisciplinary Design Master at Parsons New School in New York City, at FAAP’s Innovation-focused MBA program and many other institutions as guest lecturer. I am the author of Design Thinking Brasil, a best-selling book published by Campus-Elsevier and of the upcoming “Servir Bem para Servir Sempre”, a book about the importance of good services for both large and small businesses with practical advice on how to do it. I speak at events globally in portuguese, spanish and english about Service Design, Design Thinking, Customer Experience, Innovation Metrics and High-Performance Cultures.


Livework

After establishing myself as one of the first Service Design practitioners in Brazil, I decided to bring the first and best agency in the world to the country, becoming a partner for Livework in Latin America. For more than half a decade I’ve been involved in growing the service innovation market by doing projects that:

  • build innovation and design capacity inside organisations
  • improve the way companies provide services rethink business models from a product to a service strategy
  • help companies understand who their clients are and how they have been experiencing their offers.

EISE

The School of Service Innovation

In early 2012 I co-founded EISE, the first School of Service Innovation in the world. My role was to establish the operations needed to run the business: establishing processes, setting up management platforms and defining what kind of studying experience was possible to deliver. Besides still being a member of the board of the company I am also responsible for teaching the modules “From Products to Services”, “Service Design Practice”, “High-Performance Cultures”, “Backstage" and “Brand It Up!”.

Teaching

Since the beginning of my work in the Service Design field I have been aware that to build an inexisting market in the country I had to prepare other professionals so they could help build it too. From the first Design Thinking course in all of Latin America, founded at São Paulo’s renowned business school ESPM to DTBRontheRoad, an workshop based on my first book, and my very own Innovation School EISE, teaching has been an important part of my life. It keeps me connected to new people, new content and pushing me to become a better professional.

In the US I have taught to Transdisciplinary Design Master students at Parsons New School in New York City. In Brazil, besides teaching at before-mentioned EISE and ESPM, I teach at FAAP’s Innovation Management MBA and have been invited as a guest lecturer for graduate to phD programs all over the country.
